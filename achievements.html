List of achievements

Awarded SMVD University Silver Medal (April 2018)

Though I completed my BTech in May 2016, the convocation (which we had forgotten) was organized in April 2018. Thankfully, I was awarded University Silver Medal by the Governor of J&K in this convocation. This medal was awarded for securing first rank (mainly CGPA wise) in my batch of electronics & communication engineering.

Waitlisted for Erasmus Mundus Masters Scholarship (February 2017)

I was selected to participate in the Erasmus Mundus Master in PERvasive Computing and COMmunications for sustainable development (PERCCOM) for the 2017­-2019 edition. However, I could not make it to the main list for a Partner Country Scholarship. Thus, I was waitlisted and remained so.

Awarded NTHU International Student Scholarship (July 2016)

In July 2016, I was selected as a recipient of NTHU (National Tsing Hua University) International Student Scholarship for Master at Institute of Communications Engineering. For information per se, NTHU is one of the best universities in Taiwan, established in 1956. It is located in Hsinchu, which is known as Taiwan’s Silicon Valley. Most importantly, the Tsing in the name of the University is pronounced as ching.

Shortlisted for TIGP-International Internship Program (April 2016)

The dream of pursuing a foreign internship during my undergrad was fulfilled after the completion of my undergrad. I was shortlisted for the coveted TIGP-IIP 2016 to intern in the group named Social Networks and Human Centered Computing (SNHCC) at Academia Sinica Taiwan. Fortunately, I was one of the 6 students from India and 60 students across the world, who were shortlisted for this program. In this internship, I realized the importance of Mathematics in research, the significance of spices in food, and the role of pronunciation in speaking.

Awarded SMVD University Merit Scholarship (July 2013 - July 2015)

During my undergrad at Shri Mata Vaishno Devi University (SMVDU), I was awarded scholarship under Student Merit cum Means Scholarship Scheme (SMMSS). This scholarship was provided to top 10% students of the class, who had secured a CGPA greater than 8.50. Thus, SMVDU provided me with financial assistance during my first semester to fifth semester of my BTech.

Awarded FFE Scholarship and HDFC ECSS Scholarship (July 2012 - May 2016)

In the sophomore year of my Bachelor of Technology (BTech), I was awarded scholarship by Foundation For Excellence (FFE). This scholarship was awarded on the basis of CGAP in my BTech, AIEEE Rank, performance in 10th and 12th, and family income. Thus, FFE provided me with financial assistance during all the four years of my BTech. Apart from this, I was also a recipient of HDFC Educational Crisis Scholarship Support (ECSS) for the last two years of my BTech.

Shortlisted for Ekalavya Summer Internship at IIT Bombay (March 2015)

While I was a junior undergrad, I was actively looking for a foreign internship. Though I could not make it to any of these, I was shortlisted for the prestigious Ekalavya Summer Internship Under the guidance of Professor D. B. Phatak in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at IIT Bombay. This internship in the summer of 2015 turned out to be a milestone for my career.

Shortlisted for Summer Research Fellowship at NIT Silchar (March 2014)

While I was a sophomore, I was shortlisted for the summer research Fellowship at NIT Silchar.

Scored 95% in Maths in Senior Secondary School Examination (May 2011)

I scored 95% in Maths in the Senior Secondary School Examination (12th standard). This examination was conducted by CBSE.

Awarded the Certificate of Merit by CBSE (August 2009)

In my 10th examination, I had secured 98% in Maths and 97% in Science, along with 98% in Hindi. Thus, I was awarded a certificate of merit from CBSE for being among the top 0.1% of successful candidates in Secondary School Examination 2009 in Hindi and Science.

Secured first rank in the batch in secondary school examination (May 2009)

In 2009, I secured 96.20% in the Secondary school examination (10th) conducted by CBSE (Central Board of Secondary Education). Thankfully, I secured first rank in my batch of 80 students at JNV Muzaffarpur and was placed among the top 50 students across Bihar and Jharkhand. This was the second time when my father was happy with my performance.

Shortlisted for the first batch of JENESYS (December 2007)

In 2007, when I was struggling to understand the intricacies of Maths and Science of 9th standard, I was shortlisted for the very first batch of JENESYS (Japan ‐ East Asia Network of Exchange for Students and Youths). Thus, we (a total of 72 Indian students) had been to Japan for 10 days in December 2007. Thanks to the generous Japan Government for sponsoring our trip and special thanks to the liberal Indian Government for allowing us to be a part of this exchange program. This was the very first time, when my father was happy with my accomplishment.
